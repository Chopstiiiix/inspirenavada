Shipping should be a spectator sport.

InspireNavada started in 2024 with one online hackathon, forty-one entrants and a spreadsheet for a leaderboard. The spreadsheet is gone. The idea it stood for isn't: shipping should be a spectator sport, and nobody should have to build alone.

What we believe

Proof beats pedigree. A public, timestamped record of what you've shipped says more than any CV line. Everything on the platform — standings, ranks, write-ups — exists to build that record.

Competition is a doorway, not a wall. People arrive for prize money and leave with collaborators. We design for that arc on purpose: public boards to pull you in, circles and build nights to keep you.

Young doesn't mean junior. Most of our builders are students, self-taught, or in their first job. Four of the last ten hackathon podiums were solo entries from people under twenty-three.

How we sustain it

Individual accounts are free, forever, with no data resale and no ads. The platform is funded by companies that sponsor hackathons and hire from the boards — they pay because the signal is real, which is exactly why we keep it clean.
